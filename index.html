About BoroGrlzSk8

Beginnings

In April, 2025, I (Cailey) went to the Murfreesboro Skate Park for the first time. The whole way there, I felt anxious. When we arrived, I spent 10 minutes in the car with my fiance freaking out about how many people were skating and how embarassed I was.

I saw so many men, young and old, but not a single woman. Eventually, I got out of the car, but I was so intimidated that I didn't even skate. The next time I showed up, I actually skated. I saw the first female skateboard I had ever seen in my life.

The third time, I came alone, and I sat in the car for about 10 minutes again, contemplating if I should just leave. I saw the same girl from the last time there again, and I told myself, 'Well, at least there is one girl here.'

That's when I knew - I needed to do something about the lack of women in the skate community. I hadn't been in the community long at all, but I immediately noticed it was a problem. I knew that if I felt that way, I bet a lot of girls and women felt the same.

I discussed the situation with my best friend (Mikala) and described an idea I had to start a girls skate club to try and encourage more women to come to the park. She was on board and created our instagram for us. From there, we both decided to throw our all

into it. And that's how Boro Grlz Sk8 was born. Our vision was always to inspire girls and women to get out there and gain the confidence to skate.
